🧠 What is Insight Maker?

Insight Maker is a free, web-based simulation environment for system dynamics, agent-based models, and causal loop diagrams. It uses intuitive building blocks: stocks (accumulations), flows (rates), converters (auxiliary variables) and connectors. You can simulate complex environmental systems — from carbon cycles to wildlife populations — without writing code.

🔄 System dynamics in a nutshell: Stocks change through inflows and outflows. Feedback loops (reinforcing / balancing) drive behavior. The interactive models below reproduce this logic with real-time graphs — exactly like a mini Insight Maker playground.

🌾 Invasive Plant Spread · Adaptive Control

Logistic growth + human intervention — balancing feedback loops

🌀 Reinforcing: growth (more N → faster spread) | Balancing: carrying capacity & control
📘 Model equation: dN/dt = r·N·(1 - N/K) - u·N. Initial N₀ = 10 acres.
🧩 Insight Maker structure: Stock "Infested area", inflow "Recruitment", outflow "Removal", converters r, K, u.
⚙️ How these models work in Insight Maker
🔵 Stocks & Flows: In both models, the main stock (phosphorus or infested acres) accumulates or depletes. Insight Maker uses “stock” nodes and “flow” pipes. Our interactive sliders represent converters that directly influence flow rates.
🔄 Feedback at work: In the lake model, higher P accelerates outflow (balancing). In invasive species, density-dependent growth (carrying capacity) creates a balancing loop, while control effort adds another balancing intervention.
📊 Real-time simulation: Insight Maker runs simulations instantly. Our tutorial reproduces this using numerical integration (Euler) — you adjust parameters and the graph updates live, showing trajectories over 50 years.

🌎 Environmental science application: Eutrophication management (reduce P loading) & invasive species prevention (increase control or reduce growth) are classic policy scenarios. Use the sliders to explore trade-offs: lower loading → cleaner lakes; higher control intensity → faster eradication of invasives.
