Ecotourism Development Plan

Rizal, Southern Palawan | BS Environmental Science

A modular offline interactive guide for group workshops – aligned with the Strategic Environmental Plan for Palawan (RA 7611) and E-NIPAS Act (RA 11038). Focused on the Municipality of Rizal, its protected landscapes, coastal areas, and indigenous communities.

📘 How to use this guide: You are an environmental science consultancy team hired by the Palawan Council for Sustainable Development (PCSD), the Municipal LGU of Rizal, and the local Indigenous Peoples Organization (IPs – Palaw’an and Tagbanua). Work in groups (3–5 members). Complete the modules step by step. Use printed maps, sticky notes, and calculators. Final output: a one-page Ecotourism Management Plan tailored to Rizal.

Module 1: Site Selection & Pre‑Assessment – Rizal, Southern Palawan

Objective: Evaluate potential ecotourism sites within Rizal using Palawan’s zoning system (Core, Buffer, Multiple Use) and the municipality’s unique geography – including the Rizal Protected Landscape, coastal barangays, and river systems.

Scenario: The Municipality of Rizal has three candidate sites for ecotourism development:
1) Rizal Protected Landscape (RPL) – a key biodiversity area with lowland forest, endemic species, and ancestral domain of the Palaw’an people.
2) Canipaan River & Estuary – a mangrove-lined river with potential for kayaking and birdwatching.
3) Coastal Barangay (e.g., Punta Baja or Taburi) – sandy beaches, seagrass beds, and coral reefs.

Module 3: Carrying Capacity – Applying PCSD Guidelines in Rizal

Objective: Calculate physical, real, and effective carrying capacity using DENR‑PCSD guidelines, adapted for Rizal’s forest trails or river systems.

Scenario: You are designing a nature trail inside the buffer zone of the Rizal Protected Landscape. Trail length = 1,200 meters. Group spacing required = 50 m (to avoid disturbance to wildlife). Operating hours: 6:00 AM – 2:00 PM (8 hrs). Average visit time = 2.5 hours (round trip with interpretation).

Step 1 – Physical Carrying Capacity (PCC)
Groups at one time = 1,200 m ÷ 50 m = _______ groups.
Rotations per day = 8 hrs ÷ 2.5 hrs = _______ rotations (round down).
PCC = _______ groups × _______ rotations = _______ persons/day.

Step 2 – Correction factors (Rizal context)
- Rainy days (factor 0.6) – access road becomes impassable.
- Breeding season of endemic birds (factor 0.8) – trail closed during critical months (March–May).
- Social factor: some trails pass near Palaw’an sacred sites; only 70% of the trail can be used for tourism (0.7).
Total correction factor = 0.6 × 0.8 × 0.7 = _______
Real Carrying Capacity (RCC) = PCC × total correction factor = _______ persons/day.

Final recommended daily visitor limit: ____________ persons/day

Discussion: How do you incorporate Indigenous Peoples’ sacred sites into carrying capacity calculations? What monitoring tools can be used to ensure the limit is not exceeded?
