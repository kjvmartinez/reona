📈 The Core Problem in One Image

💰 Damages ▲     vs     🛠️ Abatement costs ▲
[Marginal damage curve ↑ with temperature]
→ Efficient point where both meet

Greatest market failure in history (Nicholas Stern).
You measure biophysical impact (e.g., 2°C = 1m sea rise).
Economics asks: How much should we spend to stop it?

3 Foundational Truths

  • Stock pollutant: CO₂ stays centuries → emissions today matter in 2100.
  • Global & irreversible: Emissions anywhere → damage everywhere.
  • Deep uncertainty: Tipping points (Amazon dieback, ice sheet collapse).
🌡️ 420 ppm CO₂ → locked-in warming for decades

🏭 The GHG Externality

Negative production externality: Emitters pay nothing for climate damages.

📉 Marginal Private Cost (MPC) < Marginal Social Cost (MSC)
⚖️ Without policy: Q_market (high emissions)
🎯 Efficient outcome: Q_social (lower emissions)

Result: massive overproduction of fossil fuels → too much CO₂.

⏳ Discounting – The Most Controversial Number

Future $1M damage – how much to pay today?

Present Value = Future Damage / (1+r)t
🔴 High r (5-7%)
Future looks cheap → act slowly (Nordhaus, DICE)
🟢 Low r (1.4%)
Future matters → act now (Stern Review)

ENVI SCI reality: Low r is ethical for climate – your grandchildren matter as much as you.

💰 Social Cost of Carbon (SCC)

Definition: Dollar damage from emitting 1 additional ton CO₂ today.

  • US EPA (2024): ~$190/ton CO₂
  • Academic range: $50 – $1,000/ton

How it's built: Integrated Assessment Models (IAMs) → climate + economic modules.

⚠️ $190/ton means gas should be ~$1.70/gal more expensive.

⚙️ IAMs – The Scientists’ Hammer

Famous: DICE (Nordhaus), PAGE (Stern), FUND.

What they do: Emissions → concentration → temperature → economic damages.

Criticism for ENVI SCI: Damage functions assume smooth losses → they cannot capture tipping points (ice sheet collapse, mass extinction).
➡️ SCC is likely severely underestimated.

🛡️ Mitigation vs. Adaptation

✅ Mitigation
Reduce emissions (solar, EVs)
Global public good
High upfront cost, delayed benefit
🏝️ Adaptation
Sea walls, drought crops
Local private benefit
Immediate protection

Econ principle: Need both. Underinvest in mitigation → adaptation becomes impossibly expensive.

💰 Policy Tool #1 – Carbon Pricing

  • Carbon tax: Set price, let quantity adjust (cost certainty).
  • Cap-and-trade: Set quantity, let price adjust (emissions certainty).

Where revenue goes? Carbon dividend (check to households) or green infrastructure.

Real-world: EU ETS ~€80/ton, Canada carbon tax ~CAD $65/ton.

📜 Policy Tool #2 – Beyond Pricing

  • Regulations: fuel economy standards, coal bans
  • Subsidies: solar tax credits, EV rebates (best paired with carbon tax)
  • R&D spending: Climate tech is a public good → markets underinvest

No single policy works – you need a policy mix tailored to political reality.

🚫 The Free Rider Problem

Game theory: Each country gains 100% of abatement costs but avoids only 1/200th of global damages.

🧩 Nash equilibrium: Everyone free rides → catastrophic warming.

Solutions: Paris Agreement (pledge & review), Carbon Border Adjustments (CBAM).

⚖️ Distributional Justice

Top 10% emitters (global North) cause most problem. Bottom 50% (global South) face 90% of impacts.

🌍 Loss & Damage finance (COP28) | Differentiated targets: Developed countries net-zero earlier.

Equity is not optional – it's central to climate economics.

📚 Case Study: Stern vs. Nordhaus

  • Stern (2006): Act now – inaction = 5-20% GDP loss. Discount rate 1.4%.
  • Nordhaus (2018 Nobel): Gradual warming to 3.5°C. Discount rate 5%.

Who was right? Stern’s ethics won in policy (Paris 1.5-2°C).
Lesson: Economics is not value-free – your discount rate is a moral statement.

📌 Summary & Key Equations

  • Externality: MSC > MPC → Pigouvian tax = SCC
  • Discounting: Low r = act fast, high r = delay
  • Policy trinity: Price carbon + regulate + invest in R&D
  • Final truth: Avoid catastrophic, not just optimal temperature.
