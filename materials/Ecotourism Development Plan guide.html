Ecotourism Development Plan

Palawan Context | BS Environmental Science

A modular offline interactive guide for group workshops – aligned with the Strategic Environmental Plan for Palawan (RA 7611) and E-NIPAS Act (RA 11038).

📘 How to use this guide: You are an environmental science consultancy team hired by the Palawan Council for Sustainable Development (PCSD) and a local government unit (LGU). Work in groups (3–5 members). Complete the modules step by step. Use printed maps, sticky notes, and calculators. Final output: a one-page Ecotourism Management Plan.

Module 3: Carrying Capacity – Palawan Standards

Objective: Calculate physical, real, and effective carrying capacity using DENR‑PCSD guidelines.

Scenario: A mangrove boardwalk in Coron, length = 800 m. Group spacing required = 50 m (to avoid disturbing wildlife). Operating hours: 7:00 AM – 3:00 PM (8 hrs). Average visit time = 1.5 hours.

Step 1 – Physical Carrying Capacity (PCC)
Groups at one time = 800 m ÷ 50 m = _______ groups.
Rotations per day = 8 hrs ÷ 1.5 hrs = _______ rotations.
PCC = _______ groups × _______ rotations = _______ persons/day.

Step 2 – Correction factors (Palawan context)
Rainy days (0.6) × Nesting season (0.7) = _______
Real Carrying Capacity (RCC) = PCC × 0.6 × 0.7 = _______ persons/day.

Final recommended visitor limit: ____________ persons/day

Discussion: Why are correction factors necessary in Palawan’s marine protected areas? How would you enforce limits without a ticketing system?
