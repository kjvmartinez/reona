Bantay Klima

Establishing a Hybrid Weather Monitoring Network Across External Campuses of Palawan State University

College of Sciences, PSU Prof. Ronald Edilberto A. Ona, PhD 24 Months Climate Monitoring IoT / Smart Sensors Disaster Preparedness

I. Rationale

Palawan is increasingly exposed to climate variability, including typhoons, erratic rainfall, and droughts. Current weather monitoring systems lack the localized resolution needed for effective decision-making, research, and community resilience.

Bantay Klima proposes a hybrid monitoring network combining commercial weather stations and DIY IoT-based sensors to generate real-time, localized climate data across Palawan State University's external campuses.

II. Objectives

  • Deploy commercial weather stations in selected PSU campuses
  • Install IoT-based smart weather sensors across sites
  • Develop a centralized real-time data management platform
  • Generate localized climate datasets for research and policy
  • Enhance institutional capacity in IoT and data analytics
  • Support climate adaptation and disaster preparedness initiatives

III. Project Components

1. Commercial Weather Stations
High-accuracy, calibrated systems Temperature & Humidity Rainfall & Wind Solar Radiation Reference Stations
2. DIY IoT Smart Sensors
ESP32 / Raspberry Pi Platforms Low-Cost Sensor Integration Solar-Powered Units Wireless Connectivity
3. Data Management System
Cloud / Local Server Infrastructure Real-Time Dashboard Historical Analysis Database
4. Capacity Building
Faculty & Student Training Integration into Academic Programs

IV. Implementation Plan

Phase Activities
Phase 1 Planning, site selection, and procurement
Phase 2 Installation of weather stations and sensors
Phase 3 System integration and dashboard development
Phase 4 Testing and calibration
Phase 5 Training and academic integration
Phase 6 Monitoring and evaluation

V. Expected Outputs

Installed Hybrid Weather Monitoring Systems Operational Real-Time Data Platform Localized Weather Datasets Technical Documentation

VI. Expected Outcomes

  • Improved climate data availability and resolution across Palawan
  • Enhanced research and data analytics capability at PSU
  • Strengthened disaster preparedness in campus communities
  • Increased student technical competencies in IoT and environmental monitoring

VII. Sustainability Plan

  • Integration into the Environmental Analytics Laboratory at PSU
  • Partnerships with government agencies and research institutions
  • Continuous funding through grants, programs, and institutional support

VIII. Risk Management

Risk Mitigation Strategy
Equipment damage from weather events Protective enclosures and proper site selection
Connectivity issues in remote campuses Hybrid communication systems (Wi-Fi + cellular)
Maintenance constraints Training local personnel for routine upkeep
Data inconsistency across sensors Regular calibration against reference stations

IX. Conclusion

Bantay Klima positions Palawan State University as a leader in environmental monitoring and climate resilience through a scalable, hybrid system integrating commercial and IoT technologies. The project advances PSU's research agenda while directly contributing to the safety and preparedness of communities across Palawan.
