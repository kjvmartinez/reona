Bantay Hangin

A Proof-of-Concept IoT System for Real-Time Indoor Air Quality Monitoring

College of Sciences, PSU Research & Development 6–12 Months Environmental Monitoring Public Health Smart Systems

1. Rationale and Background

Indoor air quality (IAQ) is a critical determinant of human health, productivity, and environmental safety. In enclosed environments such as classrooms, laboratories, offices, and healthcare facilities, poor air quality contributes to respiratory illnesses, reduced cognitive performance, and increased long-term health risks.

In the Philippine context — particularly in geographically isolated and climate-vulnerable regions such as Palawan — access to real-time, affordable IAQ monitoring systems remains limited. Existing commercial solutions are often cost-prohibitive and lack adaptability for localized deployment and academic research applications.

The Bantay Hangin Project addresses this gap through a low-cost, scalable IoT system for real-time indoor air quality monitoring, aligned with national priorities on digital transformation, environmental sustainability, and public health resilience.

2. Project Description

The project involves the design, development, and deployment of a prototype IoT-based indoor air quality monitoring system using a Raspberry Pi platform integrated with environmental sensors. The system enables real-time data acquisition, processing, visualization, and alert generation to support evidence-based environmental management.

Core Components
Raspberry Pi (Edge Computing) PM2.5 / PM10 Sensors CO₂ & VOC Sensors Temp & Humidity Sensors Wi-Fi / Cellular Connectivity Web-Based Dashboard

3. Objectives

  • Develop a functional IoT-based prototype for indoor air quality monitoring
  • Enable real-time data collection and visualization
  • Demonstrate feasibility of low-cost environmental monitoring systems
  • Support research, instruction, and extension initiatives at PSU

4. Methodology

  1. 1
    Design and Integration — Hardware assembly, sensor selection, and calibration against reference instruments.
  2. 2
    System Development — Software programming, firmware configuration, and web dashboard creation.
  3. 3
    Pilot Deployment — Installation in selected indoor environments within PSU campus facilities.
  4. 4
    Testing and Validation — Performance benchmarking and data accuracy verification.
  5. 5
    Data Analysis — Visualization, interpretation, and documentation of baseline IAQ datasets.

5. Expected Outputs

Operational IAQ Monitoring Prototype Real-Time Data Visualization Platform Technical Documentation & System Design Baseline Indoor Air Quality Datasets

6. Expected Outcomes and Impact

  • Improved awareness of indoor environmental quality among campus stakeholders
  • Data-driven decision-making in facilities and environmental management
  • Enhanced research and instructional capability within the College of Sciences
  • Foundation for smart campus and smart city initiatives in Puerto Princesa City

7. Alignment with CHED Priorities

  • Advancement of research and innovation in higher education
  • Integration of digital technologies — IoT and data analytics
  • Promotion of sustainable and resilient communities
  • Strengthening HEIs as knowledge and innovation hubs

8. Sustainability and Scalability

The project is designed for scalability across campuses and institutions. Its modular architecture enables expansion into a network of environmental monitoring systems, supporting long-term integration into smart campus infrastructure and regional environmental analytics initiatives.

9. Conclusion

The Bantay Hangin Project represents an innovative and practical approach to addressing indoor air quality challenges through accessible and scalable technology. As a proof-of-concept initiative, it establishes the foundation for broader environmental monitoring systems and contributes to national goals on sustainability, digital transformation, and public health.
