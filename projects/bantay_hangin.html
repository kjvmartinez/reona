Bantay Hangin Project

CHED-Style Concept Proposal | Palawan State University

Project Title: Bantay Hangin: A Proof-of-Concept IoT System for Real-Time Indoor Air Quality Monitoring

Proponent: College of Sciences, Palawan State University

Project Type: Research and Development (Proof-of-Concept)

Implementation Period: 6–12 Months

Priority Area: Environmental Monitoring, Smart Systems, Public Health

1. Rationale and Background

Indoor air quality (IAQ) is a critical determinant of human health, productivity, and environmental safety. In enclosed environments such as classrooms, laboratories, offices, and healthcare facilities, poor air quality contributes to respiratory illnesses, reduced cognitive performance, and increased long-term health risks.

In the Philippine context, particularly in geographically isolated and climate-vulnerable regions such as Palawan, access to real-time, affordable IAQ monitoring systems remains limited. Existing commercial solutions are often cost-prohibitive and lack adaptability for localized deployment and academic research applications.

The Bantay Hangin Project addresses this gap through the development of a low-cost, scalable Internet of Things (IoT) system for real-time monitoring of indoor air quality. The project aligns with national priorities on digital transformation, environmental sustainability, and public health resilience.

2. Project Description

The project involves the design, development, and deployment of a prototype IoT-based indoor air quality monitoring system using a Raspberry Pi platform integrated with environmental sensors.

Core Components

  • Raspberry Pi as edge computing unit
  • Air quality sensors (PM2.5/PM10, CO₂, VOCs)
  • Environmental sensors (temperature, humidity)
  • Wireless connectivity (Wi-Fi/cellular)
  • Web-based monitoring dashboard

The system will enable real-time data acquisition, processing, visualization, and alert generation, supporting evidence-based environmental management.

3. Objectives

  • Develop a functional IoT-based prototype for indoor air quality monitoring
  • Enable real-time data collection and visualization
  • Demonstrate feasibility of low-cost environmental monitoring systems
  • Support research, instruction, and extension initiatives

4. Methodology

  • Design and Integration: Hardware assembly and sensor calibration
  • System Development: Software programming and dashboard creation
  • Pilot Deployment: Installation in selected indoor environments
  • Testing and Validation: Performance benchmarking
  • Data Analysis: Visualization and interpretation

5. Expected Outputs

  • Operational IoT-based IAQ monitoring prototype
  • Real-time data visualization platform
  • Technical documentation and system design
  • Baseline indoor air quality datasets

6. Expected Outcomes and Impact

  • Improved awareness of indoor environmental quality
  • Data-driven decision-making in facilities management
  • Enhanced research and instructional capability
  • Foundation for smart campus and smart city initiatives

7. Alignment with CHED Priorities

The Bantay Hangin Project supports key CHED strategic priorities, including:

  • Advancement of research and innovation in higher education
  • Integration of digital technologies (IoT, data analytics)
  • Promotion of sustainable and resilient communities
  • Strengthening of higher education institutions as knowledge hubs

8. Sustainability and Scalability

The project is designed for scalability across campuses and institutions. Its modular architecture enables expansion into a network of environmental monitoring systems, supporting long-term integration into smart campus infrastructure and regional environmental analytics initiatives.

9. Conclusion

The Bantay Hangin Project represents an innovative and practical approach to addressing indoor air quality challenges through accessible and scalable technology. As a proof-of-concept initiative, it establishes the foundation for broader environmental monitoring systems and contributes to national goals on sustainability, digital transformation, and public health.
